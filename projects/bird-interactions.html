0. Where it started

This project was the result of our biggest CSC111 assignment. After spending the entire year learning various data structures and algorithms in Python, we finally had the opportunity to apply everything we learned to a real-world problem of our choice.

For the first time, we were completely on our own; no starter code, no step-by-step instructions. Just guidelines: identify a meaningful problem, find a dataset, and develop a solution using concepts we learned throughout the year. However, there was one constraint: we had to use trees and/or graphs to represent some core part of our project.

After analyzing various datasets and brainstorming potential problems to solve, we decided to focus on the interactions between bird species in Toronto. We were inspired by the fact that birds are often overlooked in urban environments, yet they play a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance. By analyzing their interactions, we hoped to uncover insights that could inform conservation efforts and urban planning.
